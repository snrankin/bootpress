    You may be a candidate for weight loss surgery if you have: 

    • A BMI of 35 or higher along with other existing medical problems
    • Type 2 Diabetes
    • High blood pressure/heart disease
    • Osteoarthritis of weight-bearing joints
    • Sleep apnea/respiratory problems
    • Gastroesophageal reflux/Heartburn
    • Depression
    • Infertility
    • Urinary stress incontinence
    • Menstrual irregularities

    Bariatric Treatment Options at
    St. Joseph Medical Center

    Adjustable Gastric Banding

    A band is placed around the upper part of the stomach, which reduces the stomach’s size so that patients eat smaller meals and still feels full. This procedure is adjustable and reversible.

    Gastric Bypass

    A small stomach pouch is created by separating the top of the stomach from the rest of the stomach. The small intestine is also divided, and the bottom end of the small intestine is attached to the newly formed stomach pouch. This limits the amount of food your stomach can hold and the calories your body can absorb while also suppressing appetite. 

    Revision Weight Loss Surgery

    This procedure is done on patients who have previous undergone a bariatric weight loss surgery in which they have either had complications or been unable to achieve significant weight loss results.

    Sleeve Gastrectomy

    Seventy-five percent of the stomach is removed during this permanent weight loss surgery procedure. This results in a stomach that is similar in shape to a banana and allows patients to feel a sense of fullness from smaller meals.
